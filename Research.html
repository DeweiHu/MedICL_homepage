Research Areas

Semantic Segmentation

  Segmentation is a very common task that helps the clinical practitioners with diagnosis and surgery. We focus on developing learning-based solutions for the segmentation of different tissue in diverse image modalities (i.e. MRI, CT, OCTA etc.). To address the lack of manual annotations for medical images, semi-supervised and unsupervised approaches are proposed.

Selected publications:
  • Cats: Complementary CNN and Transformer Encoders for Segmentation
  • Multiple Sclerosis Lesion Segmentation with Tiramisu and 2.5 D Stacked Slices
  • LIFE: A Generalizable Autodidactic Pipeline for 3D OCT-A Vessel Segmentation
  • Segmentation of kidney stones in endoscopic video feeds

Domain Generalization & Adaptation

  Deep learning algorithms are vulnerable to domain shift which severely impedes the broader deployment. Medical images are often distributed diversely even for data in the same modality. Therefore, we developed domain adaptation and generalization techniques to help the deep model work on out-of-distribution data from different sites or modalities. The performance is usually evaluated by conducting a downstream task such as segmentation.

Selected publications:
  • Domain Generalization for Retinal Vessel Segmentation with Vector Field Transformer
  • Unsupervised cross-modality domain adaptation for segmenting vestibular schwannoma and cochlea with data augmentation and model ensemble

Computer-Aided Diagnosis

  The brain MRI illustrates the textural and morphological change of tissue caused by pathologies. Analyzing brain MRI enables diagnosis or prediction of many diseases (e.g. Parkinson's disease) which makes it possible for early treatment and prevention. We developed patch-based analysis for accurate prediction and classification. Further, this approach is able to improve the deep learning algorithms.

Selected publications:
  • Anatomical texture patterns identify cerebellar distinctions between essential tremor and Parkinson's disease
  • Multi-scale graph-based grading for Alzheimer’s disease prediction
  • Patch-Based Abnormality Maps for Improved Deep Learning-Based Classification of Huntington’s Disease

Image Stitching

  Our lab is dedicated to advancing medical imaging analysis, with a particular focus on 4D placenta ultrasound image stitching. Ultrasound imaging is a valuable tool for prenatal care, providing clinicians with essential information on fetal development and potential health issues. However, traditional 2D and 3D ultrasound images do not provide a comprehensive view of the complex anatomical structures of the placenta, which can limit diagnostic accuracy. To address this limitation, we are researching innovative techniques for 4D ultrasound image stitching, which will enable clinicians to visualize and analyze the placenta in its entirety. Our research involves developing advanced algorithms that can automatically register and stitch multiple 3D ultrasound images into a single high-resolution 4D image, providing a detailed view of the placenta's structure and function over time. Our ultimate goal is to contribute to the advancement of medical imaging analysis and improve the quality of prenatal care for expecting mothers and their babies.

Image Denoising

  Due to the imaging technique, some medical image modalities (e.g. OCT, ultrasound) suffer from high noise that can severely degrade the visibility of some important anatomy. Therefore, image denoising is an essential pre-processing step for either diagnosis or other downstream task like segmentation. Since it is impossible to get the noise-free ground truth, the unsupervised learning methods are favorable for this problem. We proposed to implement the deep diffusion model that can reduce the speckle noise of different level in retinal OCT images.

Selected publications:
  • Unsupervised denoising of retinal OCT with diffusion probabilistic model
  • Retinal OCT Denoising with Pseudo-Multimodal Fusion Network

Harmonization

  Medical image harmonization has become increasingly crucial due to the collection of medical imaging data from diverse sources, which may have variations in imaging parameters and quality. With the aid of deep learning and the availability of large-scale medical imaging datasets, medical image harmonization has become essential in ensuring consistency and reliability in medical imaging analysis, crucial for accurate diagnoses and effective treatment decisions. In our lab, we are laser-focused on solving the challenging sMRI image harmonization problem for patients with Huntington's disease. Our objective is to contribute to the progression of medical image harmonization and ultimately enhance the accuracy and consistency of medical imaging analysis, which can significantly impact healthcare outcomes.
