Welcome to Medical Image Computing Lab

MISSION

Research in MedICL is directed towards the development of novel methodology for quantitative medical image analysis, with focus on neuroimaging, obstrtric imaging and ophthalmology applications. With the help of the vast amount of data and boosting computation power, artifitial intelligence has achieved remarkable performance in almost all the image analysis tasks. Our goal is to push the boundary forward so that the algorithms can provide better aid for the real clinical procedure.
We are in the Department of Computer Science and Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering. We are also affiliated with Vanderbilt Institute for Surgery and Engineering (VISE). Therefore, we have great opportunity to collaborate with researchers with strong biomedical and clinical background. For more details of our work, please visit here.

RESEARCH PROJECTS

Lesion Inpainting

Inpainting lesions is an important preprocessing task for algorithms analyzing brain MRIs of multiple sclerosis (MS) patients, such as tissue segmentation and cortical surface reconstruction. We propose new deep learning approaches for this task.

  • MS lesion
    MLMI 2020

Domain Generalization & Adaptation

To tackle the domain shift of data acquired from different sites, we proposed DG and test-time adaptation methods that enable the deep models to work on unseen domains.

  • domain generalization
    MIDL 2022, SPIE 2023
  • domain adaptation
    MLCN 2022

Semantic Segmentation

Segmentation is a very common task that helps the clinical practitioners with diagnosis and surgery. We presented segmentation algorithms that are suitable for different organs/tissue.

  • subcortical
    MLCN 2020, SPIE 2021, ISBI 2022
  • multiple sclerosis
    MICCAI 2019, SPIE 2023
  • retinal vessel
    MICCAI 2021
  • kidney stones
    SPIE 2022

Surface-based Analysis

Surface-based methods are more robust than their volumetric counterparts since they are not sensitive to noise and topological defects. We conducted surface-based approaches for cortical thickness measurement and cortical segmentation.

  • cortical thickness measurement
    SPIE 2021, Frontiers in Neuroimaging 2022
  • cortical segmentation

Computer Aided Diagnosis

The development of machine learning algorithms has great potential to enable early diagnosis of many diseases. We proposed patch-based grading method to detect disease from brain MRI.

  • Parkinson's disease
    Human Brain Mapping 2021
  • Alzheimer's disease
    MedIA 2020
  • Huntington's disease
    ISBI 2020, MICCAI 2020

Image Denoising

Due to the imaging technique, some modalities (e.g., OCT, ultrasound) suffer from high noise that can degrade the visibility of important anatomy. We leverage the generative models (e.g., GAN, DDPM) for image denoising.

  • retinal OCT
    OMIA 2020, SPIE 2022
  • saphenous nerve ultrasound image
